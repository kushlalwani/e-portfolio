NFL Fantasy Prediction Model with Rookie Emphasis

This project explores a data-driven approach to predicting NFL fantasy football performance, with a particular emphasis on rookie players. Using five years of historical data, separate modeling pipelines were developed for rookies and veterans.

The project incorporates feature engineering techniques such as strength of schedule and team rankings, and evaluates multiple machine learning models to generate predictions and rankings. The final output provides insights that can support real-world decision-making in fantasy sports.

Reflection

This project highlights both my technical and analytical abilities, as well as my ability to communicate complex work. One of the most important things I learned was how to structure a large technical project into a clear narrative that explains the problem, approach, and results. I also gained experience working with real-world data challenges such as missing data and noisy outcomes. This project demonstrates my ability to apply data science techniques in a practical context and communicate the results in a way that is understandable and useful.
